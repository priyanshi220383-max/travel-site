Gangotri Dham
⭐ 4.8 / 5 Tourist Rating

Gangotri is the origin of the sacred River Ganga and one of the four Char Dhams of Uttarakhand. Situated at 3,048 metres in the Uttarkashi district, the temple is dedicated to Goddess Ganga and stands beside the roaring Bhagirathi River. Legend holds that King Bhagirath performed penance here to bring Ganga from the heavens to earth. The destination is surrounded by dense Himalayan forests, glaciers and snow-covered peaks that create an atmosphere of profound peace. The nearby Gaumukh glacier — the actual source of the Ganga — is a 20 km trek from the town and is one of the most dramatic pilgrimage treks in India.

State Uttarakhand
Best Time May – June & September – October
Altitude 3,048 m (9,997 ft)
Famous For Gangotri Temple, Origin of River Ganga
Trek to Gaumukh 18 km (Gangotri to Gaumukh glacier)
Trip Duration 2 – 4 Days
Nearest Railway Uttarkashi (100 km)
Nearest Airport Jolly Grant Airport, Dehradun (250 km)

Featured Guides

Places To Visit

Gangotri Temple, Bhagirath Shila, Surya Kund, Pandava Gufa, Kedartal Lake and the Gaumukh Glacier snout.

Gaumukh Trek Guide

The 18 km trek from Gangotri to Gaumukh passes through Chirbasa and Bhojbasa meadows. Altitude permit required from Forest Dept.

Ganga Aarti

The evening Ganga aarti at Gangotri ghat is a deeply moving ritual. Attend at sunset when the priests offer lamps to the roaring river.

Kedartal Trek

A challenging 25 km trek from Gangotri to Kedartal lake at 4,750 m — a turquoise glacial gem reflecting the Jogin massif.

Uttarkashi Stopover

Base town with Vishwanath temple, local markets and river rafting on the Bhagirathi. A natural overnight stop before Gangotri.
