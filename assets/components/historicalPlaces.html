Historical Places of Uttarakhand
⭐ 4.6 / 5 Tourist Rating

The historical places of Uttarakhand tell a story that most travellers never encounter — the rise and fall of the Chand dynasty that ruled Kumaon for over six centuries (7th–18th century AD), their magnificent forts at Champawat and Lohaghat, the ancient Nagnath and Baleshwar temples they built, the old Tibetan trade routes through Pithoragarh's Dharchula that linked India to Lhasa, and the British colonial presence at Abbott Mount. Champawat — the first capital of the Chand kings before they moved to Almora — is a sleepy town today, but its fort ruins, the Baleshwar temple complex and the surrounding villages carry the weight of six hundred years of mountain kingdom history. Jim Corbett's early hunting grounds around Champawat add a colonial literary dimension.

Featured Guides

Champawat — First Chand Capital

Champawat served as the capital of the Chand kings before they moved to Almora in 1563. The town still has ruins of the royal fort, the Baleshwar temple complex and old nagari-script copper plates recording land grants.

Baleshwar Temple Complex

A 10th–12th century Shiva temple built by the Chand kings, remarkable for its stone carvings. The adjacent Ratneswar and Champawati Durga temples complete a stunning heritage precinct that few tourists visit.

Pithoragarh — Frontier Fort

Pithoragarh's 18th-century fort (built by the Gorkhas who briefly conquered Kumaon) overlooks a dramatic Himalayan valley. The town was also a key node on the Tibet–India trade route through Dharchula.

Patal Bhuvaneshwar Cave

A sacred limestone cave complex near Gangolihat with stalactite formations worshipped as the 108 petals of Sheshnag's hood. Mentioned in the Skanda Purana — pilgrims crawl through narrow passages to reach the inner sanctum.

Abbott Mount — British Retreat

A perfectly preserved 1920s British hill settlement 11 km from Lohaghat — stone bungalows, a tiny church, a club and pine forests. Named after John Harold Abbott, it is one of the least-altered British retreats in India.
