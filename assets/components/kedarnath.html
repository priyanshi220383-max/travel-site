Pilgrimage Destination · Jyotirlinga
Kedarnath
⭐ 4.9 / 5 Tourist Rating

Kedarnath is one of the holiest shrines in Hinduism and one of the twelve Jyotirlingas of Lord Shiva. Perched at an awe-inspiring altitude of 3,583 metres in the Garhwal Himalayas, the ancient stone temple dates back to the 8th century and is attributed to Adi Shankaracharya. The dramatic landscape — ringed by the Kedarnath, Kedar Dome and Bharatekuntha peaks — makes this a journey of both spiritual and natural magnitude. The famous 16 km trek from Gaurikund to the temple passes through waterfalls, meadows and glacial terrain that humbles every pilgrim.

State Uttarakhand
Best Time May – June & September – October
Altitude 3,583 m (11,755 ft)
Famous For Kedarnath Jyotirlinga Temple, 12 Jyotirlingas
Trek Distance 16 km (Gaurikund to Temple)
Trip Duration 3 – 5 Days
Nearest Railway Rishikesh (225 km)
Nearest Airport Jolly Grant Airport, Dehradun

Featured Guides

Places To Visit

Kedarnath Temple, Bhairavnath Temple, Gandhi Sarovar Lake, Shankaracharya Samadhi, Triyuginarayan Temple.

Trekking Guide

The 16 km Gaurikund–Kedarnath trail via Rambara and Linchauli. Pony, doli and helicopter options available for those who need them.

Helicopter Service

Direct helicopter service from Phata, Sirsi and Guptkashi helipads. Book early — slots fill months in advance during peak season.

Food Guide

Gaurikund has simple dhabas serving hot dal-rice and maggi. The temple committee also serves free langar prasad to all pilgrims.

Scenic View Points

Gandhi Sarovar for the glacier lake, Kedarnath peak for a panoramic sunrise, and Chorabari glacier for ice-field views up close.
