Hemkund Sahib
⭐ 4.9 / 5 Tourist Rating

Hemkund Sahib is one of the holiest Sikh shrines in the world, perched beside a pristine glacial lake at a breathtaking altitude of 4,329 metres in the Chamoli district of Uttarakhand. The Gurudwara Shri Hemkund Sahib commemorates the place where the tenth Sikh Guru, Guru Gobind Singh, meditated in a previous life. The trek to Hemkund passes through the legendary Valley of Flowers National Park — a UNESCO World Heritage Site — making this a combined spiritual and natural pilgrimage unlike any other. The sight of the seven snow-capped Saptarishi peaks reflected in the still glacial lake is unforgettable.

State Uttarakhand
Best Time June – September (open season)
Altitude 4,329 m (14,203 ft)
Famous For Gurudwara Hemkund Sahib, Valley of Flowers
Trek Distance 13 km from Govindghat (6 km Govindghat–Ghangaria + 7 km Ghangaria–Hemkund)
Trip Duration 3 – 5 Days
Nearest Railway Rishikesh (270 km)
Nearest Airport Jolly Grant Airport, Dehradun (300 km)

Featured Guides

Hemkund Sahib Gurudwara

The sacred gurudwara sits beside Hemkund Lake at 4,329 m. Pilgrims take a dip in the icy lake before entering the gurudwara for ardas. Langar (free meals) served to all.

Valley of Flowers Trek

A UNESCO site at 3,658 m blooming with 500+ species of wildflowers — brahmakamal, blue poppy, cobra lily. Open July–September for peak bloom.

Ghangaria Base Camp

The key base village at 3,050 m serving both Valley of Flowers and Hemkund Sahib. Has basic guesthouses, dhabas and the GMVN rest house.

Govindghat

The starting point for both treks. The famous Gobind Ghat Gurudwara is here. Helicopter service from Govindghat to Ghangaria available.

Wildlife & Flora

Spot Himalayan black bear, musk deer, snow leopard (rare) and hundreds of alpine bird species on the Valley of Flowers trail.
