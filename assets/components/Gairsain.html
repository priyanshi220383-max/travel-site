Gairsain
⭐ 4.6 / 5 Tourist Rating

Gairsain is a small but historically and politically significant town in the Chamoli district of Uttarakhand, sitting at an altitude of 1,750 metres at the confluence of the Garhwal and Kumaon divisions. In 2020, it was declared the summer capital of Uttarakhand — a decision that fulfilled a decades-long demand of the hill people who wanted the seat of power closer to their mountains. Beyond politics, Gairsain is surrounded by dense oak and rhododendron forests, terraced hillside villages, ancient Shiva temples and the kind of unhurried Pahari life that has disappeared from most Himalayan towns. The Bhराल river valley below it and the views of the Trishul and Nanda Devi ranges from the ridges above make it a destination that rewards the traveller who wanders off the beaten path.

Featured Guides

Uttarakhand's Summer Capital

Declared the summer capital of Uttarakhand in 2020, Gairsain's Bhararisain Vidhan Sabha building sits on a hilltop with commanding views of the Garhwal and Kumaon ranges. The decision ended a 20-year political debate.

Ancient Shiva Temples

The Gairsain area has several old stone Shiva temples built by the Chand and Parmar dynasties, mostly unvisited by tourists. The Laxmi Narayan temple and old Shiva shrine in the main village are well preserved.

Nauti — Start of Raj Jaat

Nauti village, 12 km from Gairsain, is the starting point of the Nanda Devi Raj Jaat Yatra — held every 12 years, considered the world's largest Himalayan pilgrimage. The village Nanda Devi temple is the ceremonial origin point.

Oak & Rhododendron Forests

Dense forests of banj oak and buransh (rhododendron) surround Gairsain. In March–April the rhododendrons bloom red across the hillsides. Forest trails lead to quiet ridgeline viewpoints with unobstructed Himalayan panoramas.

Pahari Village Life

The villages around Gairsain — Dewal, Tharali, Nauti — have stone houses with carved wooden windows, women in traditional pichhaura dress, terraced mandua fields and weekly haats (markets) that feel unchanged for generations.
