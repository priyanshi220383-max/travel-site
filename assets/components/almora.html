Almora Heritage
⭐ 4.7 / 5 Tourist Rating

Almora is the cultural and intellectual heart of the Kumaon Himalayas, perched on a 5 km long horse-shoe shaped ridge at 1,638 metres. Founded by the Chand dynasty in 1568, the town retains its colonial-era bazaars, centuries-old temples, traditional stone-paved lanes and distinctive Kumaoni architecture with elaborately carved wooden facades. Artists, writers and philosophers — from Swami Vivekananda to Timothy Leary — have been drawn to Almora's unique energy. Today it is celebrated for its Aipan folk art, brass craft, bal mithai sweet, and the panoramic Himalayan views stretching from Nanda Devi to Panchachuli that unfold at every turn.

Featured Guides

Lala Bazaar & Mall Road

The famous old bazaar with slate-roofed shops selling brass utensils, Aipan prints, Almora shawls and the legendary bal mithai. Walk it slowly — every shop has a story.

Nanda Devi Temple

The 16th-century Nanda Devi temple is the spiritual centre of Almora. The intricate stone carvings and the brass idol of Goddess Nanda are extraordinary examples of Katyuri craftsmanship.

Bright End Corner

The most celebrated viewpoint in Almora — a ridge-top garden where at dawn the entire Himalayan range from Nanda Devi to Panchachuli glows gold in the rising sun.

Kasar Devi Temple

A small Shiva temple 8 km from Almora that sits on a powerful geomagnetic zone. In the 1960s–70s it attracted Bob Dylan, Cat Stevens, Timothy Leary and Allen Ginsberg.

Binsar Wildlife Sanctuary

28 km from Almora, Binsar offers the finest Himalayan panorama in Uttarakhand — 300 km of unbroken Himalayan skyline from Kedarnath to Nepal's peaks, seen from Zero Point.
