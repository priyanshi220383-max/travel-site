Yamunotri
⭐ 4.7 / 5 Tourist Rating

Yamunotri is the first dham in the Char Dham Yatra and the source of the sacred River Yamuna. Nestled in the Garhwal Himalayas at an altitude of 3,293 metres, the temple is dedicated to Goddess Yamuna, the twin sister of the god of death Yama. The journey to Yamunotri itself — a 6 km trek from Janki Chatti through rhododendron forests, rushing streams and thermal springs — is as spiritually charged as the destination. The natural hot spring Surya Kund at the temple complex, where pilgrims cook rice and potatoes in the scalding waters as offerings, is one of the most unique rituals in Indian pilgrimage culture.

State Uttarakhand

Featured Guides

Yamunotri Temple

The main shrine dedicated to Goddess Yamuna, rebuilt in the 19th century by Maharani Guleria of Jaipur. Opens each May at the auspicious Akshaya Tritiya.

Surya Kund Hot Spring

The Surya Kund is a scalding natural hot spring where pilgrims cook offerings wrapped in cloth. Water temperature reaches near boiling point.

Janki Chatti Trek

The 6 km trail from Janki Chatti winds past waterfalls, bridges and prayer flags. Ponies and dolis available for those who need assistance.

Hanuman Chatti

A scenic confluence town 13 km before Janki Chatti. Visit the Hanuman temple and the meeting point of Hanuman Ganga and Yamuna rivers.

Barkot Base Town

The comfortable base town for Yamunotri. Has hotels, markets and a relaxed Himalayan vibe. Ideal overnight stop before the trek.
