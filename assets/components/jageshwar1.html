Ancient Pilgrimage Site · Jyotirlinga
Jageshwar Dham
⭐ 4.8 / 5 Tourist Rating

Jageshwar is one of the most remarkable ancient temple complexes in India — a cluster of 124 stone temples from the 7th–12th century AD, hidden deep inside a sacred deodar cedar forest in the Almora district. Dedicated primarily to Lord Shiva, Jageshwar is considered one of the twelve Jyotirlingas and holds immense significance in the Shaiva tradition. The temples, built in the Nagara architectural style by the Katyuri dynasty, range from small shrines to elaborate multi-storey temples with intricate stone carvings. The entire complex is protected by the Archaeological Survey of India and has a meditative, forest-enveloped atmosphere unlike any mainstream pilgrimage site.

Featured Guides

The Temple Complex

124 temples spanning 7th–12th century AD. The Dandeshwar and Lakulisha temples are the largest. Every stone here has a carved deity or mythological narrative.

Deodar Cedar Forest

The temples are wrapped inside a mature deodar forest that creates a cathedral-like atmosphere. The forest is considered sacred and no trees are felled here.

Shravani Mela

The month-long Shravani Mela (July–August) draws lakhs of devotees from across Kumaon. The temple is spectacularly lit and the atmosphere is electric.

Archaeological Museum

The ASI museum adjacent to the temples holds stone sculptures, copper plates and artefacts excavated from the site. Essential context for understanding the complex.

Vriddha Jageshwar

A 5 km trek above the main complex leads to the older 'Vriddha Jageshwar' (Elder Jageshwar) shrine — quieter, more atmospheric and rarely visited by tourists.
