Ranikhet
⭐ 4.6 / 5 Tourist Rating

Ranikhet — literally 'Queen's Field' — is a serene British-era cantonment town at 1,829 metres in the Almora district, administered by the Kumaon Regiment of the Indian Army. The town's wide, pine-shaded avenues, colonial bungalows with red-roofed verandahs, apple and apricot orchards, and the famous Upat Golf Course (one of Asia's highest golf courses) carry an unhurried Edwardian charm that has barely changed in a century. From Chaubatia Gardens, the entire Himalayan arc — Trishul, Nanda Devi, Nanda Kot, Panchachuli — stretches across the horizon like a painted frieze. It is a town built for slow mornings, long walks and quiet contemplation.

Featured Guides

Chaubatia Gardens

A 600-acre government fruit farm and garden at 1,800 m with orchards of apples, peaches, plums and apricots. The Himalayan panorama from here — Nanda Devi, Trishul, Panchachuli — is outstanding.

Upat Golf Course

One of Asia's highest golf courses at 1,800 m, run by the Kumaon Regiment. The 9-hole course is surrounded by towering oak and rhododendron trees with Himalayan peaks as a backdrop.

Jhula Devi Temple

A famous Durga temple 7 km from town, known for the thousands of brass bells tied by devotees as vows. The tinkling of bells in the mountain air is deeply atmospheric.

Kumaon Regiment Museum

A well-curated regimental museum chronicling the Kumaon Regiment's history — from colonial campaigns to modern wars. Medals, photographs, weapons and battle accounts.

Bhalu Dam & Tarikhet

A quiet reservoir 3 km from town ideal for picnics and birdwatching. Tarikhet village nearby has a famous Bhagat Singh connection — he lived here briefly in 1927.
